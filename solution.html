My Solution

To solve the problem of the ever-growing food demand,
the problem must be solved from multiple fronts.

Where the focus needs to be:

  • One of the first steps towards effectively feeding the world's population is changing
    its dieting habits.     According to Kyle Davis, if crop production is lower than the demand
    level, a volumetric diet increase would be at fault. Davis also mentions that anywhere from
    1.8-3.1 billion additional people can be fed from a change in dieting changes alone.
  • Maximization of crop yield is the most advantageous way to provide food to ten billion people.
    One of the current solutions for a greater crop yield is the use of GMO crops. Currently, GMOs create
    a 22% increase in crop yield. To increase the effect of GMOs, it is necessary for the world to
    research and develop more advanced GMOs.
  • One third of all food produced is wasted through the current food supply chain according to the
    Food and Agricultural Organization. At every link in the chain, there is evidence of food waste.
    These weak links in the food supply chain must be targeted and eliminated.
  • The UK government has made multiple recommendations as to how ten billion will be fed. Their point
    which was stressed the most is sustainability of crops into the future. While certain crops might
    survive in today's climate, they may not in tomorrow's. Dr. Richards states that in the future, pests
    and diseases that affect crop yield are constantly evolving to resist the pesticides and GMOs that
    are used by farmers.
